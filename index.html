Tips for Financial Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity is a critical concern for individuals and organizations, especially in an age where digital threats are increasingly sophisticated. Financial institutions are responsible for protecting personal financial information, but individuals also play a crucial role in safeguarding their data. While no system is foolproof, understanding common threats like customer account takeovers and theft of personal financial information can help mitigate risks.

Customer account takeovers occur when criminals steal usernames and passwords to gain unauthorized access to personal accounts, including online financial accounts. Attackers may use synthetic identities, combining real and fake information, to create new identities and open fake accounts.

Account takeovers can start with phishing emails that appear legitimate but ask for sensitive information like account numbers or passwords. Cybercriminals may also engage in social engineering, pretending to be representatives from financial institutions to obtain personal information. Data breaches and the sale of stolen credentials on the dark web are other common avenues for account takeovers.

How to Minimize the Risk of Account Takeovers

To minimize the risk of account takeovers and protect personal financial information, individuals should:

Watch What You Click

Avoid clicking on suspicious links or responding to unsolicited emails. Verify information directly on the financial institution's website.

Use Strong Passwords

Use unique passwords for each account and change them regularly. Consider using a password manager to keep track of passwords.

Enable Multifactor Authentication (MFA)

Use MFA for an added layer of security, requiring two or more factors for authentication.

Maintain Device Security

Install and update security software with antivirus, anti-spam, and spyware detection features. Keep your devices updated with security patches.

Use Your Own Device

Avoid using public computers or unsecured Wi-Fi networks for financial transactions.

Browse Carefully

Ensure websites are secure (https://) and log out after each session.

Be Cyber Safe When Using Wi-Fi

Use secure, encrypted networks and avoid using public Wi-Fi for financial transactions.

Review Correspondence

Regularly review account activity and statements for unauthorized transactions.
